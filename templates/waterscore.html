Manure Storage and Application

Adding manure additives such as digestive enhancers, acidifying agents, or adsorbents helps stabilize nitrogen in manure, reducing ammonia emissions and leaching, and thereby lowering nitrogen pollution.


Applying manure to a growing crop or just before crop establishment in spring, rather than in autumn, aligns nutrient release with plant uptake, reducing nitrogen losses through leaching and runoff.


Avoiding the application of excess nitrogen ensures that crops only receive the amount they can absorb, preventing surplus nitrogen from leaching into groundwater or running off into surface water.


Grazing
